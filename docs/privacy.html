Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-14 · App: Kibo — Custom Keyboard for iPhone (com.douchaojun.kibo)

Kibo (“the App”, “we”) is a custom keyboard for iPhone. We designed Kibo so that privacy is enforced by the system, not just promised in a document. This policy explains exactly what the App does and does not do with your information. By using the App you agree to this policy.

1. The Kibo keyboard does not have Full Access

Kibo’s keyboard extension is built with Full Access disabled (RequestsOpenAccess = false). We never ask you to enable Full Access.

Because Full Access is not requested, iOS prevents the keyboard from communicating over the network at all. The keyboard extension has no network access capability. It cannot send anything, anywhere, to us or to anyone else.

2. We do not collect your keystrokes

  • We do not collect, log, store, or transmit anything you type — no characters, words, sentences, passwords, messages, or clipboard contents.
  • Everything you type is passed directly to the app you are typing into, exactly as iOS intends. Nothing is retained by Kibo.
  • We do not build any profile, typing history, or dictionary from your input.

3. The only data Kibo stores

The App stores a small amount of preference data so the keyboard behaves the way you configured it. This is the complete list:

  • The theme ID you selected.
  • Your sound effect on/off setting.
  • Your haptic feedback on/off setting.
  • Your auto-capitalization on/off setting.
  • Your recently used emoji, so the emoji panel can show them first.

All of this is stored locally on your device inside a shared App Group container belonging to Kibo. It never leaves your device. It is not uploaded, not backed up to our servers (we have no servers for it), and not shared with anyone.

4. No analytics, no advertising, no tracking

  • Kibo contains no third-party analytics SDKs.
  • Kibo contains no advertising SDKs and shows no ads.
  • We do not track you across apps or websites, and we do not sell or share data with data brokers.
  • We do not collect device identifiers, location, contacts, or any personally identifiable information.

5. Accounts

Kibo has no user accounts. There is no sign-up, no sign-in, no email address, and no profile.

6. Purchases and subscriptions

Kibo may in the future offer optional paid features or a subscription. If it does, all purchases are processed by Apple through the App Store. Apple handles the transaction and your payment details. We never see, receive, or store your payment information (no card number, no billing address). Your purchase status is provided to the App by Apple’s in-app purchase system.

7. Account Deletion

Kibo has no accounts and collects no personal data, so there is no account to delete and no server-side data to erase.

The only data that exists is the small set of local preferences listed in Section 3, stored on your own device. Deleting the Kibo app from your iPhone permanently deletes all of that data. Nothing remains with us, because nothing was ever sent to us.

If you have any question about deletion, contact support@douhouse.com.

8. Children’s privacy

Kibo does not knowingly collect any information from children, and in fact collects no personal information from any user regardless of age.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. If the App’s behavior ever changes — for example, if a future feature would require network access — we will update this page before that change ships, and the “Last updated” date above will reflect it.

10. Contact us

For any privacy question, contact: support@douhouse.com

Summary: Kibo’s keyboard runs without Full Access and therefore has no network access. It does not collect, store, or transmit anything you type. The only data stored is your theme, sound/haptics/auto-capitalization settings, and recently used emoji — kept locally on your device and never sent anywhere. No analytics, no ads, no accounts. Deleting the app deletes everything. Contact: support@douhouse.com
